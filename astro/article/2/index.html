Making history at UW Health
Liver

Even though more than two decades have passed since John gave part of his liver to his cousin, Cindy, he still becomes emotional when talking about it. Back in 2000, Cindy was suffering from hepatitis C and needed a liver transplant. John had read an article about a new option for living liver donation and asked Cindy’s UW Health caregivers whether he might donate part of his liver to her.

At the time, a living liver donation program didn’t exist in Madison, but surgeons were currently working with their first donor. John kept checking back, and by Christmas of 2000, the team agreed to test him. He was a match, and John gave Cindy the gift of life on April 20, 2001.

Now a retired chaplain, John takes every opportunity to talk about the importance of organ donation. “It’s such an incredible gift that you can give another person,” he says.
